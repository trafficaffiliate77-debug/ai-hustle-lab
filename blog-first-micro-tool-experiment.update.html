I Built an AI Micro-Tool in 7 Days for $0 — The Honest Report

Week 1 results from my first AI side hustle experiment. Real numbers, real mistakes, and what I'd do differently next time.

The idea

I built an online character counter tool — the kind people search for when writing tweets, meta descriptions, or essays with character limits.

Why this idea?

  • Real demand: "Character counter" gets ~500K searches/month
  • Weak competition: Top results are ugly, outdated, ad-heavy
  • Buildable fast: 3-4 days with AI
  • Clear monetization: Display ads + affiliate links for writing tools
  • $0 startup: Free hosting, free AI tools, free design

Week 1 stats (honest)

23 Visitors
$0 Revenue
15h Time invested
$0 Money spent

Yes, those numbers are tiny. But they tell the real story of starting from zero.

The 7-day timeline

Day 1 Research & validation — Searched Google, analyzed top 10 results, identified weaknesses. 3 hours.
Day 2 Design & UX plan — Sketched wireframes in Canva. Listed 5 features competitors lacked. 2 hours.
Day 3-4 Build with AI — Used Claude to write the tool logic. HTML/CSS/JS generated. 6 hours.
Day 5 Testing & fixes — Mobile responsiveness, dark mode, edge cases. 2 hours.
Day 6 Deploy & SEO — Cloudflare Pages, Google Search Console, sitemap. 1.5 hours.
Day 7 Content & submission — Wrote FAQ + About page. Submitted to Bing. 30 min.

The exact stack I used

Everything free:

  • Code: Claude (free) wrote the HTML/CSS/JS
  • Hosting: Cloudflare Pages (free tier)
  • Domain: Using free Cloudflare subdomain (buying custom domain at month 2 if traffic picks up)
  • Design: Canva free for logo and favicon
  • Analytics: Google Analytics 4 (free)
  • SEO: Google Search Console + Bing Webmaster Tools

Total cost: $0

What went wrong

Lesson 1: I almost shipped a bad idea. My first choice was a "word counter" — but those have heavy competition (Microsoft Word, Google Docs). Pivoted to character counter on day 2. Validation saved me weeks.
Lesson 2: Claude free tier ran out twice. Had to wait 4 hours for quota reset. Plan content in batches to avoid this.
Lesson 3: SEO takes time. Week 1 = 23 visitors. Realistic month 1 = 100-300 visitors if I keep publishing content.
Lesson 4: Mobile responsiveness broke twice. Always test on actual phone before declaring "done."

What's working

The tool itself works great. Clean UI, fast, mobile-friendly. Visitors who land on it stay (low bounce rate).

The problem isn't the product. The problem is traffic. I need more content and backlinks.

Week 2 plan

  • Write 3 supporting articles (SEO content)
  • Apply for Google AdSense
  • Cross-link to AI Hustle Lab (this site)
  • Submit to 10 free directories
  • Start tracking keyword rankings

Realistic expectations

Here's what success looks like at different stages:

  • Month 1: 100-500 visitors, $0-5 revenue
  • Month 3: 1,000-5,000 visitors, $20-100 revenue
  • Month 6: 10,000+ visitors, $200-500 revenue
  • Month 12: 50,000+ visitors, $1,000-3,000 revenue

Yes, it's slow. Yes, it compounds. The first 3 months are about building the asset. The next 9 are about harvesting it.
