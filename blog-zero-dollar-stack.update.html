How I Replaced $237/Month in AI Tools with Free Alternatives

I was paying $237/month for AI subscriptions. Then I tested free alternatives for each one. Here's every swap I made — what worked, what flopped, and what I actually missed.

The wake-up call

Two months ago I added up my software subscriptions and felt sick.

$237/month. That's $2,844 per year. For tools I was using maybe 30% of their features.

So I did an experiment: try replacing each paid AI tool with a free alternative. Use both for 30 days. Keep whichever wins.

Here's what happened.

Swap #1: Jasper AI → Claude Free

❌ Jasper AI ($49/month) — CANCELLED

What I used it for: Marketing copy, email sequences, landing page drafts

What I replaced it with: Claude free + a saved Google Doc with 10 prompt templates

Result: Claude does 90% of what Jasper did. The 10% I lose? Custom brand voice training. Not worth $49/month for my stage.

Verdict: Saved $588/year. Don't miss it.

Swap #2: Surfer SEO → Perplexity Free

❌ Surfer SEO ($89/month) — CANCELLED

What I used it for: NLP optimization, content scoring, keyword research

What I replaced it with: Perplexity free (for research) + manual competitor analysis + Google Search Console (free)

Result: My rankings didn't change. The NLP optimization Surfer provides is real but not worth $89/month when I'm publishing 2 articles/week.

Verdict: Saved $1,068/year. Will re-subscribe at $200+/month revenue.

Swap #3: ConvertKit → Beehiiv Free

❌ ConvertKit ($29/month) — CANCELLED

What I used it for: Email sequences, automations, broadcasts

What I replaced it with: Beehiiv free tier (welcome sequence, forms, recommendations)

Result: Beehiiv's free tier has everything I need at my list size (under 2,500 subs). The 0% revenue cut vs ConvertKit's fees is huge.

Verdict: Saved $348/year. Will upgrade when I hit 2,500 subs.

Swap #4: Notion AI → ChatGPT Free

❌ Notion AI ($10/month) — CANCELLED

What I used it for: Quick summaries, rewrites, translations

What I replaced it with: ChatGPT free (already had a tab open)

Result: Notion AI was convenient (built into my docs) but redundant. I was already using ChatGPT for everything else.

Verdict: Saved $120/year. Zero loss.

What I kept (still worth paying for)

✅ Canva Free (kept)

Not AI, but essential. Free tier is enough for 90% of design work. Built my entire lead magnet in Canva free.

✅ Buffer Free (kept)

Schedules social posts across 3 channels. Saves 2+ hours/week. Free tier is enough.

✅ Google Drive + Docs (kept)

Free. Everywhere. Done.

Annual savings

$2,844

What I actually lost

Honesty time. Here's what I gave up:

1. Speed. Free AI tiers have wait times. Claude free caps at ~10 messages/day during peak hours. Plan content in batches.

2. Brand voice training (Jasper). If you have a specific brand voice, Jasper's training is better. For me, generic → I-edit is fine.

3. Advanced SEO data (Surfer). For competitive niches, Surfer's NLP analysis helps. For low-competition keywords, it doesn't matter.

4. Advanced automations (ConvertKit). Beehiiv's free tier is basic. If you need complex sequences, you'll hit walls.

The framework: when to pay for AI tools

Here's my rule now:

Only upgrade when the tool directly earns more than it costs.

  • If a paid tool helps me earn $200/month extra → I pay for it
  • If a paid tool just makes me slightly faster → I use the free version
  • If I'm just starting → Everything free until $100/month revenue
